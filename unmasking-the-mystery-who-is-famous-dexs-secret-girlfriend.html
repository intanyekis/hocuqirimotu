Famous Dex Early Life & Career

Famous Dex is a rapper from America who was born on September 6, 1993, in Harlem, near Manhattan, New York City. After relocating there, he spent his childhood in the Englewood neighborhood on the South Side of Chicago, Illinois.

Dex showed an interest in music from a young age and decided to pursue it after the death of his mother in September 2014. He began his career as a dancer in his friend’s music video, but he quickly gained fame after changing his name to “Famous” and creating his own hip-hop music.

In 2014, Dex released his first song, “Bottle After Bottle,” which has been viewed over 197,226 times on YouTube. He is well known for his popular songs, including “Pick It Up,” “Japan,” and “Rich Forever.”

Dex’s debut album, “Dex Meets Dexter,” was released in 2018 and peaked at number 12 on the Billboard 200 chart. The album’s singles, “Pick It Up” and “Japan,” also performed well on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, peaking at numbers 54 and 28, respectively.

Despite Dex’s success in the music industry, he has faced several controversies throughout his career. In 2016, he was involved in a domestic violence incident that resulted in legal consequences and the cancellation of his endorsement deal with Puma.

Despite the setbacks, Famous Dex has continued to pursue his passion for music, and his talent has earned him a dedicated fan base.
