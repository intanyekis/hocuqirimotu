Jeannie Mai Biography, Ex-Husband Freddy Harteis, Divorce, Net Worth, Parents

Jeannie Mai is a well-known American actress, television personality, makeup artist, and fashion veteran. She has been the host and co-host of several TV shows like Fantasy Character which she hosted in 2006 on USA Network and What Do I Look Like? on Style Network in 2004. She is currently co-host of the Fox talk show The Real which debuted on July 15, 2013.

Over the years, Jeannie Mai has proven that she is capable of handling anything thrown at her; especially at the end of his marriage. Many people don’t realize how strong she is, but the truth is that irreconcilable differences in a marriage always mean disaster and Jeannie can attest to that fact. Her marriage to ten-year-old Freddy Harteis fizzled out due to a difference of opinion on the issue of childbearing.

Here’s more information you didn’t know about the talk show host.

Jeannie May: Biography,

Jeannie Camtu Mai was born on January 4, 1979 in San Jose, California and is the eldest of her parents. She grew up in California with two younger brothers, Daniel and Dennis. Growing up, she practiced her original religion, Buddhism, but later changed to the Christian faith.

She had her secondary education from Milpitas High School and then a degree in communications from De Anza College. Belonging to a large family, the rising star started working early in life; by the age of 16, Mai was already getting into interviews and testing makeup bags owned by her mother, a local celebrity.

Jeannie May: Career

At the age of 18, Mai landed his first job as a makeup artist with MAC Cosmetics while living in San Jose, California. She gradually progressed in her career until she was considered competent enough to work for celebrities. Later, MTV Total Demand Live employed her as their celebrity makeup stylist and so did others, including Good Day Sacramento and KCAL Los Angeles.

In 2003, after auditioning for several hosting gigs, she landed a job, co-hosting Stir on the international channel. After that, she was employed to host her own music countdown on California Music Channel. Later, she landed the job of entertainment producer and reporter for The Daily Mixx on WB.

His first primetime job was in 2005 on the USA Network’s Fantasy Character . When she finally moved to Los Angeles, she began hosting segments related to entertainment news and fashion on several networks such as NBC, E!, Lifetime and TLC. TLC 10 Years Younger finally offered her a spot on their beauty team.

2008 was a special year for Jeannie Mai, she not only hosted the Miss America Reality Check but also Where’s Your Style and the Special Guy Makeover. The same year, the cosmetics brand Never Accept Ordinary made Mai their spokesperson.

In 2009, the Style Network’s fashion makeover show What I Look Like made her its host and during weekends she was on NBC’s Extra TV with correspondent and weekend host Mario Lopez. She became the host of the Miss Universe pageant in 2011 and also collaborated with NBC weight loss pageant, The Biggest Loser as a fashion consultant. Mai was among the guest judges during the first round of Asia’s Next Top Model in 2012.

Since 2014, Jeannie has hosted several shows including Style Pop on the Style Network, The Real on FOX and Steampunk’d on GSN.

She has also received several brands, including Woolite, Macy’s Splenda, Colgate, Avon, Yoplait, Ford, The Limited, David’s Bridal, Microsoft and Biogen.

Parents

Jeannie Mai has Vietnamese and Chinese heritage, thanks to her parents. Her father, James Mai, is Vietnamese while her mother, Olivia TuTram Mai, is Chinese. Growing up, Jeannie made sure to stay in touch with both sides of her heritage.

Ex-Husband, Freddy Harteis & Divorce

October 2017 was not a happy month for Jeannie Mai and her husband, Freddy Harteis. Their decision to end their ten-year-old marriage was made public and the reason cited in the divorce papers was irreconcilable differences over the issue of procreation.

Jeannie had always been against the idea of ​​children and she made that clear from the start of their relationship, even before marriage. However, as he got older, Freddy changed his mind. In other words, the divorce was inevitable.
