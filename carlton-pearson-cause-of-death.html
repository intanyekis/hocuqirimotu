Carlton Pearson Cause of Death

It is confirmed that he is dead. Cancer is listed as Carlton Pearson’s cause of death. It was said that he fought the illness for a short while before meeting a tragic end. Bishop Carlton Pearson, the subject of the Netflix series “Come Sunday,” unexpectedly passed away on Sunday at the age of 70 following a brief battle with cancer. According to a Facebook post.

“We are saddened to inform you that Bishop Carlton D’Metrius Pearson, one of the most popular and influential preachers in America and around the world, who sacrificed everything for a message of unconditional love and acceptance by God, died peacefully the night of November 19, 2023, at the age of 70, after a brief battle with cancer that had returned after first defeating it 20 years ago,” the Facebook post from Pearson’s page stated.

The post further stated that Pearson died with his loved ones by his side. The Associated Press was notified by Pearson’s agent, Will Bogle, that his death occurred on Sunday night in Tulsa, Oklahoma, when he was under hospice care.

The Netflix film “Gospel of Inclusion” and Pearson’s life were portrayed in the Netflix movie “Come Sunday.” April 2018 saw the debut of the film, directed by Joshua Marston and starring Chiwetel Ejiofor as the vicious Pentecostal pastor.

In a social media video that appeared to have been shot in a hospital ward, Pearson revealed in August of this year that he had been fighting prostate cancer for almost 20 years. Thus, cancer is the reason for Carlton Pearson’s passing.

Carlton Pearson Biography

Pearson was born on March 19, 1953, in San Diego, California. While attending Oral Roberts University in Tulsa and being coached by Oral Roberts, he sang with the World Action Singers. Before we get into the specifics of Carlton Pearson’s death and obituary, we need to learn more about him.

He later became an associate evangelist with the Oral Roberts Evangelistic Association. He was licensed and ordained in the God in Christ Church. Pearson launched the Higher Dimensions Evangelistic Center in 1981, which evolved to become one of Tulsa’s largest churches.

Along with Dr. Frederick K. C. Price, he was previously one of only two African-American pastors to conduct a weekly national television preaching show that reached millions or hundreds of thousands of viewers each week. He is also known for being one of the first black ministers to hold major conferences in stadiums and arenas around the country. During the 1990s, over 6,000 people attended Pearson’s church on a weekly basis.

On the first night of his annual AZUSA Conference in 1996, Carlton Pearson was ordained as a bishop, and he was consecrated on the first night of AZUSA ’97. Pearson was invited to the White House after endorsing George W. Bush in 2000. Pearson also had one of Trinity Broadcasting Network’s most popular TV series.

Pearson also held the AZUSA Conference in Tulsa. Pearson also traversed the country, holding two-day revival gatherings. Pearson also gave T. D. Jakes, Joyce Meyer, and Donnie McClurkin a national stage and an international audience.

Pearson has also met with and spoken with former Presidents Bill Clinton and George H. W. Bush. Pearson was also a gospel vocalist who was nominated for two Stellar Awards and one Dove Award.
