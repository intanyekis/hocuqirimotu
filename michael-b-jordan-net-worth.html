American actor Michael B. Jordan has an estimated net worth of $1 million. He is best recognized for his appearance on The Wire as Wallace, as well as in All My Children as Reggie Montgomery. He is also noted for portraying the role of Vince Howard in  Friday Night Lights, as Oscar Grant in Fruitvale Station, and in Parenthood as Alex.

Michael Bakari Jordan was born on February 9, 1987 in Santa Ana, California,

USA. Prior to pursuing an acting career, Jordan worked as a model for Toys "R" Us and Modell's sporting goods.

As an actor, Michael B. Jordan debuted in the film Black and White, as well as in TV shows The Sopranos and Cosby.  On television, Jordan is noted for his recurring appearances in shows like The Wire, The Assistants, Friday Night Lights, Lie To Me, and Parenthood.  

His appearance in the 2013 film Fruitvale Station won Jordan a Hollywood Spotlight Award at the Hollywood Film Awards. In addition to Fruitvale Station, he recently worked as the voice of Cyborg/Victor Stone in the 2013 film Justice League: The Flashpoint Paradox. In 2014, he is expected to appear in the film That Awkward Moment.
