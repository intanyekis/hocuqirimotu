What was Anton Yelchin’s Net Worth?

Anton Yelchin, a Russian-American actor, had a net worth of $10 million at the time of his untimely death at age 27 in a freak car accident on June 19, 2016. He rose to fame through his roles in the “Star Trek” reboot and in films such as “Terminator Salvation.” Yelchin had his acting debut at age 10 in the independent drama “A Man is Mostly Water.” Throughout his career, he appeared in various films and TV shows, including “Law and Order: Criminal Intent” and “Curb Your Enthusiasm.” However, his breakthrough performance was in the 2007 crime drama “Alpha Dog,” where he played a character based on a real-life kidnapping victim. Yelchin became best known for portraying Pavel Chekov in the rebooted “Star Trek” movie franchise after transitioning to more mature roles.

Early Life and Career

Anton Yelchin was born in Leningrad, Soviet Union, in 1989 to Irina Korina and Viktor Yelchin, who were both figure skaters. The family left for the United States when he was six months old and were granted refugee status. Yelchin grew up in California and attended the Sherman Oaks Center for Enriched Studies before studying film at the University of Southern California. He had already started his acting career during his childhood.

Anton Yelchin’s Career Highlights

Anton Yelchin made his film debut at the age of 11 in “A Man is Mostly Water” in 2000. He had several roles in movies like “Delivering Milo,” “15 Minutes,” “Along Came a Spider,” and “Hearts in Atlantis” the following year. He also appeared in the TV series “Huff” and guest-starred in shows like “Curb Your Enthusiasm,” “Law & Order: Criminal Intent,” and “Criminal Minds.”

Yelchin played a character based on a real-life murder victim in “Alpha Dog” and also appeared in “Fierce People” and “Charlie Bartlett.” In 2009, he played the role of Pavel Chekov in the rebooted “Star Trek” franchise and also played a young Kyle Reese in “Terminator Salvation.”

In 2011, Yelchin appeared in “Fright Night” and “Like Crazy,” and lent his voice to “The Smurfs” movie. He reprised his role of Pavel Chekov in “Star Trek Into Darkness” in 2013 and also appeared in “Odd Thomas,” “Burying the Ex,” and “5 to 7.”

Yelchin starred in the independent horror film, “Green Room,” which premiered at the 2015 Cannes Film Festival. He also appeared in “The Driftless Area” and voiced a role in “Trollhunters: Tales of Arcadia.” His final films, “Porto,” “Thoroughbreds,” “Rememory,” and “We Don’t Belong Here” were all released posthumously, as he passed away in June of 2016.

A Look into Anton Yelchin’s Life

Anton Yelchin grew up with cystic fibrosis, but did not disclose it to the public. He was passionate about playing the guitar and was a member of a punk band called Hammerheads. Additionally, he had an interest in photography. During the filming of “Fierce People,” Yelchin briefly dated co-star Kristen Stewart, who later referred to him as her first heartbreak.

In 2012, Yelchin purchased a home in Studio City, California, for $945,000 when he was 23 years old. Today, his parents reside in the house.

Anton Yelchin’s Tragic Death

On June 16, 2016, Anton Yelchin was found pinned between his Jeep and a brick pillar gate post outside of his home. He was pronounced dead at the scene at the age of 27. The police determined that he was involved in a freak accident after getting out of his car to walk down his steep driveway to check the mail and lock the gate. The vehicle then rolled downhill, pinning Yelchin against the post.

Yelchin was honored following his death when “Star Trek Beyond” was dedicated to him and Leonard Nimoy, who had also recently died. Several other films and television shows that he had been involved in were also dedicated to his memory. In October of 2017, a bronze statue of Yelchin was erected at his grave in the Hollywood Forever Cemetery. A documentary about his life premiered at the 2019 Sundance Film Festival.
