Early Life and Career Beginnings

Sinbad, whose real name is David Adkins, was born on November 10, 1956, in Benton Harbor, Michigan. He grew up with his five siblings and attended Benton Harbor High School.

After completing high school, Sinbad joined the United States Air Force, where he discovered his talent for stand-up comedy. In 1981, he participated in the Air Force’s Talent Contest and showcased his unique comedic style.

Following his military service, Sinbad pursued a career in comedy, making appearances on the popular show “Star Search” and gaining recognition for his exceptional comedic skills.

Sinbad’s early life laid the foundation for his success in the entertainment industry. His experiences in the Air Force shaped his comedic abilities and provided him with the platform to launch his career.

With his natural charisma and wit, Sinbad quickly rose to prominence in the world of stand-up comedy, captivating audiences with his relatable humor and storytelling abilities.
